## The Real Problem (why it still feels wrong)

This page is designed like:

* “Here is how our system works internally”

But it needs to feel like:

* “Relax. This happens automatically while you run your business.”

Right now, it still violates **consumer best practices** in three major ways.

---

## 1. The Page Is Process-Centric, Not Outcome-Centric

### What it currently communicates

* Diagnose
* Decide
* Deploy

This is **your internal mental model**, not the user’s.

Consumers do not want to think in steps.
They want to think in **before vs after**.

### What consumers expect instead

* “We find problems”
* “We fix them”
* “Your traffic improves”

### How to fix it (non-negotiable)

**Rename the steps in consumer language**:

| Current  | Consumer-grade replacement |
| -------- | -------------------------- |
| Diagnose | We Find What’s Broken      |
| Decide   | We Fix It Automatically    |
| Deploy   | Your Traffic Improves      |

The technical explanation can live *under* that framing — but the headline must be outcome-first.

---

## 2. The Visual Rhythm Is Too “SaaS Documentation”

Even with good colors and icons, the layout still feels:

* Linear
* Dense
* Text-forward
* Symmetrical in a way that feels instructional

This is **not** how high-conversion consumer SaaS explains value.

### What’s missing

* Emotional pacing
* White space that signals ease
* Visual proof that “work is happening without you”

---

## 3. Icons Are Still Decorative, Not Meaningful

You fixed **style**, but not **function**.

Right now icons:

* Sit beside text
* Don’t convey motion, automation, or progress
* Don’t *replace* reading — they *add* to it

In consumer UX, icons should:

* Reduce reading
* Reinforce a single idea instantly
* Feel secondary to a strong headline

---

## The Correct Consumer Pattern (what this page should do)

This page should answer **one question only**:

> “Do I need to do anything?”

And the answer should be:

> “No.”

Everything else is supporting evidence.

---

## How to Fix This Page (Concrete, Actionable)

### 1. Replace the top headline entirely

**Current**

> How Arclo Works

**Replace with**

> SEO that runs on autopilot

Subhead:

> We find issues, fix them, and keep your site optimized — without you lifting a finger.

This immediately reframes the page emotionally.

---

### 2. Change the layout from “steps” to “assurance blocks”

Instead of 3 equal steps in sequence, use **3 reassurance sections**:

#### Block 1: “We handle the hard part”

* Big statement
* One sentence
* One icon
* Minimal bullets (max 2)

#### Block 2: “You don’t need to review anything”

* This is CRITICAL
* Explicitly say:

  > You never have to read reports or make decisions.

#### Block 3: “You stay in control (if you want)”

* This reduces anxiety
* Explain the “Fix it” button here conceptually

---

### 3. Reposition the icons (important)

Icons should:

* Be **above** the headline (not beside paragraphs)
* Be smaller
* Act as anchors, not illustrations

Bad:

* Icon → paragraph → bullets

Good:

* Icon
* One strong sentence
* Optional supporting line

---

### 4. Add a “Nothing required from you” callout

This page desperately needs a reassurance callout box.

Example:

> **No work required**
>
> Hermes runs automatically in the background.
> You’ll only hear from us when something improves — or if action is needed.

This is consumer SaaS gold.

---

### 5. Add a CTA that reinforces automation (not learning)

At the bottom, instead of “Learn more” vibes:

Primary CTA:

> Fix my SEO automatically

Secondary:

> See an example report (optional)

Do **not** end this page without a clear automation-first CTA.
