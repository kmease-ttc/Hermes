## Hermes — Light Mode Marketing Palette (Recommended)

### 1. Core Backgrounds (this is the biggest change)

**Primary Page Background**

* `#F8FAFC` (Very light cool gray)
* Use for: hero, main sections
* Why: feels clean, calm, SaaS-native, not stark white

**Secondary Section Background**

* `#FFFFFF` (Pure white)
* Use for: feature blocks, cards, comparison sections

**Subtle Alt Section Background**

* `#F1F5F9`
* Use for: alternating sections, “What’s included”, FAQs

---

### 2. Text Colors (readable, not aggressive)

**Primary Text (Headlines)**

* `#0F172A` (Slate 900)
* Strong but not black → avoids harsh contrast

**Body Text**

* `#334155` (Slate 700)
* Comfortable long-form reading

**Muted / Helper Text**

* `#64748B` (Slate 500)
* For subcopy, disclaimers, helper lines

---

### 3. Primary Brand / CTA Colors (keep your green, soften delivery)

**Primary CTA Button**

* `#22C55E` (Emerald 500)
* Text: `#FFFFFF`
* Hover: `#16A34A`

This keeps the “growth / SEO” signal without feeling neon or aggressive.

**Secondary CTA / Outline Button**

* Border: `#22C55E`
* Text: `#16A34A`
* Background: transparent → `#ECFDF5` on hover

---

### 4. Accent & Status Colors (used sparingly)

**Accent Blue (trust / automation)**

* `#3B82F6`
* Use for links, subtle highlights, icons

**Success / Positive Signal**

* `#10B981`

**Warning / Attention (rare)**

* `#F59E0B`

---

### 5. Card & Container Styling (important for “calm” feel)

**Card Background**

* `#FFFFFF`

**Card Border**

* `#E2E8F0`

**Card Shadow**

```css
box-shadow: 0 8px 24px rgba(15, 23, 42, 0.06);
```

Rounded corners:

* `border-radius: 12px` (minimum)
* 16px is even better for approachability

---

### 6. Header / Navigation (light, not dominant)

**Navbar Background**

* `#FFFFFF` (or `rgba(255,255,255,0.85)` with blur)

**Navbar Border Bottom**

* `#E5E7EB`

**Logo / Brand Mark**

* Keep current green
* Avoid placing on dark backgrounds

---

### 7. Footer (this is where dark is OK)

**Footer Background**

* `#020617` (Near-black slate)
* Text: `#CBD5E1`

This preserves contrast and brand seriousness **after** conversion intent is established.

* “This is easy”
* “This is safe”
* “This works quietly in the background”
* “I don’t need to understand SEO to use this”

It removes:

* Tool anxiety
* Perceived effort
* The “dashboard before signup” vibe
